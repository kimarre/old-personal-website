Kittens Are Overrated.

December 26, 2013

Moose had a little too much last Thanksgiving.

Kittens are always the first to be adopted from shelters and are super abundant on r/aww. It’s easy to list the reasons why someone would want a kitten. They’re tiny and cuddly, have squeaky little meows, tiny paws, and you get to watch them grow up into ferocious mini-beasts.

I’ve had my kitten, Moose, for about 6 months now. I brought him home from the county shelter in Bakersfield when he was only 8 weeks old. Although I would never trade my Moosey-Moose for anything, I have learned a few things that have made me believe that adopting a full grown cat probably would have been a better choice for me.

Don’t get me wrong, I know that after 8 months of owning my kitten, I’m probably desensitized to the adorable kitten stuff and am taking it for granted. Though, often times I feel that he grows up going through many of the same phases that human children go through.

He’s currently in his teenage angst stage. As far as I know, as his mom, I get the most attitude backlash after scolding him for doing things he shouldn’t be doing. Mostly in the form of feisty arm and ankle biting. Although that somehow still manages to sound rather cute, I can assure you, the abundant scars on my body would respectfully disagree.

It begins with a scolding, progresses to his little eyes dilating as if he were on kitty LSD, and ends with a viciously playful strike to my leg. One that I don’t consider to be all that playful.

I know he doesn’t mean any actual harm, but I have to say, I’m pretty excited for him to grow up and mellow out a little bit. He is a good companion when he’s calm and makes for a good personal heater during winter naps, but I look forward to a day where I’m not afraid of the tiny knives attached to his paws.
